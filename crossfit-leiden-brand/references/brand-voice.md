# CrossFit Leiden Brand Voice

Complete tone of voice and personality guidelines for all CrossFit Leiden communications.

## Brand Essence

### Who We Are
CrossFit Leiden is geen standaard sportschool. We zijn een tweede huiskamer voor ouders, een thuis voor expats, en een plek waar mensen fitter en blijer naar buiten lopen dan ze binnenkwamen.

Bij ons geen harde fitnessmuziek en spiegels vol ego's, maar een warme, internationale community met persoonlijke aandacht voor iedereen.

> "Iedereen hoort bij ons minstens één keer zijn naam tijdens een training."

### Our Philosophy
Onze gym is een **middel, geen doel.** Mensen trainen bij ons om sterker te worden voor het echte leven: voor het ouderschap, voor die hike in de Alpen, voor de energie op je werk, of simpelweg om met meer plezier te bewegen.

## Core Values

### 1. Persoonlijk
Everyone is seen. We coach with attention and know you by name. This isn't just a slogan - it's how we operate every single class.

### 2. Toegankelijk  
Everyone can participate. We don't attract athletes, but life-enjoyers. Beginners are welcome, returning exercisers are celebrated, and everyone finds their level.

### 3. Vriendschappelijk
The vibe is like a good friend: warm, honest, with humor. We create an atmosphere where people feel comfortable being themselves.

### 4. Leiderschap met een glimlach
We know what we're talking about, but keep it relaxed. Expert guidance without intimidation.

### 5. Avontuurlijk  
We live outside the box. Literally. From trail runs to kitesurfing to Murph, we embrace adventure in all its forms.

### 6. Duurzaam
We help people build habits that stick. This isn't about quick fixes or extreme transformations - it's about sustainable lifestyle change.

## Tone of Voice

### Overall Tone: Informal, Positive, Expert

We speak like we coach: with energy, humor, and an open attitude. We avoid hype and complicated jargon. We use CrossFit terms, but explain them and keep them accessible.

### Language Rules

**Do:**
- Short sentences
- Active voice
- Conversational language
- Humor where appropriate
- Explain CrossFit terminology
- Use "members", "community" or "people"
- Be specific and concrete
- Show personality

**Don't:**
- Use em-dashes (use regular dashes or commas instead)
- Say "athlete" (this excludes our target audience)
- Use fitness industry clichés
- Be preachy or condescending
- Overcomplicate things
- Use excessive emojis (only functional ones)

### Personality Archetypes

Think of our brand voice as four distinct personalities working together:

#### 1. The Good Friend
- Remembers your name and details about your life
- Pushes you just enough, knows your limits
- Celebrates your wins, big and small
- Makes you feel seen and valued

#### 2. The Guide
- Explains clearly without being condescending
- Shares knowledge generously
- Points the way without pushing
- Makes complex things simple

#### 3. The Mood Maker
- Creates an atmosphere where everyone feels at home
- Brings energy and positivity
- Uses appropriate humor
- Makes fitness fun, not intimidating

#### 4. The Adventurer
- Invites you to step outside your comfort zone
- Shares stories of challenges and adventures
- Makes the extraordinary feel accessible
- Inspires through action, not just words

## Example Phrases

### Good Examples
- "Niet trainen om te trainen, maar trainen voor het leven daarbuiten."
- "Vandaag spierpijn. Morgen trots."
- "Coach Eva fietste met ons van Parijs naar Leiden - en hielp je net ook nog met je deadlift."
- "Je komt moe binnen, je gaat trots weg."
- "Nieuwe gewoontes beginnen hier - met een glimlach, niet met stress."

### What to Avoid
- ❌ "Crush your goals!" (too aggressive)
- ❌ "Elite athletes train here" (excludes our audience)
- ❌ "No pain, no gain" (outdated, not our philosophy)
- ❌ "Transform your body in 30 days" (unrealistic, not sustainable)

- Can use more emojis (but still functional)

### Website
- Informative but warm
- Clear value propositions
- Addresses concerns and questions
- Shows personality while building trust

### Email Communications
- Personal and direct
- Clear calls to action
- Helpful information
- Appropriate level of formality based on context

- Inspiring but authentic
- Focus on lifestyle benefits

## Target Audience

### Demographics
- Average age: 40 years
- More women than men
- International, highly educated
- Not competitive, but committed
- Seeking community and lifestyle

### Psychographics
- Value quality over quantity
- Want expert guidance
- Appreciate personal attention
- Looking for sustainable change
- Interested in adventure and challenge
- Prioritize balance and enjoyment

### Pain Points We Address
- Intimidation by typical gyms
- Lack of personal attention
- Boring, repetitive workouts
- Difficulty maintaining motivation
- Wanting community connection
- Seeking lifestyle integration

### Coach Philosophy
Our coaches are guides and community builders. They have specialisms (mobility, gymnastics, outdoor), many are parents, and all are engaged. They're visible, approachable, professional - and have fun with members.

They help not just with training, but also with nutrition, habits, and recovery.

## Brand Ambition

We want to be the most inspiring gym in Leiden - recognizable by our style, felt in our atmosphere. We inspire not just our members, but also people who don't (yet) train with us. Everyone is welcome - including to the adventure outside the box.

## Writing Guidelines by Format

### Headlines
- Clear and benefit-focused
- Can be playful but not at the expense of clarity
- Active voice preferred
- Example: "YOUR ROAD TO FEELING GREAT"

### Body Copy
- Conversational but informative
- Break up long paragraphs
- Use bullet points for lists (not for everything)
- Include specific details and examples

### Calls to Action
- Direct and friendly
- Example: "START YOUR JOURNEY"
- Avoid: "Sign up now!", "Don't miss out!"

### Image Captions
- Add context or story
- Can be more casual
- Connect to broader message

## Voice in Different Languages

While most content is in Dutch, English is used for:
- International audience communications
- Social media (mix of both)
- Some marketing materials

**Dutch Guidelines:**
- Use "je" (informal you)
- Keep the same warm, friendly tone
- Translate spirit, not just words

**English Guidelines:**
- American English spelling (CrossFit standard)
- Same personality and values
- May reference international CrossFit culture

## Quality Checks

Before publishing any content, ask:
1. ✅ Does this sound like a good friend talking?
2. ✅ Would a beginner feel welcome?
3. ✅ Is it clear and jargon-free (or explained)?
4. ✅ Does it reflect our values?
5. ✅ Would we say this in person?
6. ✅ Is it authentic to who we are?

If you answer "no" to any of these, revise the content.
